A Case Study of Me

A Smooth Transition

I started my career as a packaging designer drawn by how consumers interact with packaging as a 3-D object and by how design influences their purchasing decisions. Understanding the consumer always came first before design. Design and strategy were never two separate entities to me. We help brands tell their stories, making an emotional connection with consumers. We simplify the complicated and addressed the goals of the consumer.

"Design and strategy were never two separate entities to me."

These same principles are practiced in user experience design. I found that the reasons I enjoyed packaging design were more fully realized in user experience design. What excites me the most about user experience is that it gives designers a broader reach and the chance to address everyday problems. User experience impacts our lifestyles, productivity, work flow, relationships and understanding of the world. Switching to a product designer title allowed me to capitalize on my strengths in problem solving and creative thinking.
